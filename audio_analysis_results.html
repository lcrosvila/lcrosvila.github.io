Audio Analysis Results

Understanding the Results

This analysis explores how various audio transformations affect the performance of audio classifiers. By applying transformations like low-pass and high-pass filtering at different cutoff frequencies, and downsampling at specific rates, we aim to identify key characteristics of audio that impact classification accuracy. Specifically, transformations include:

  • Low-pass filters at cutoff frequencies of 1,000 Hz, 8,000 Hz, 10,000 Hz, and 16,000 Hz, to allow frequencies below the cutoff to pass through.
  • High-pass filters at the same frequencies, allowing only the higher frequencies to pass.
  • Downsampling to 22,050 Hz and 24,000 Hz, reflecting half the sampling rates of the AI and non-AI audio (48,000 Hz and 44,100 Hz respectively).

By observing the classifiersâ€™ predictions on these altered versions of the audio, we can assess how different frequency ranges or sample rates influence classifier accuracy and gain insights into which features are significant for distinguishing between AI and non-AI generated audio.
